SEGMENT 1: THE PAID ADS LANDSCAPE FOR PSYCHIATRY IN 2026 — WHAT'S CHANGED AND WHY IT MATTERS FOR REFRESH

The paid advertising landscape for psychiatric practices has shifted dramatically in the last 24 months, and if you're still running the same Google Ads campaign you set up in 2023, you are almost certainly burning money.

Let's start with Google Search Ads — the highest-intent, highest-converting channel for psychiatric practices. In 2026, the average CPC (cost per click) for healthcare practices across search ads is between $3 and $7, but psychiatry-specific terms are at the high end of that range. Keywords like "psychiatrist near me," "ADHD doctor near me," and "anxiety medication online" are competitive — and getting more competitive as well-funded nationals pour money in.

Here's what Refresh Psychiatry faces specifically: Talkiatry raised a $210 million Series D in February 2026. They have 800+ full-time psychiatrists and operate in 45 states. That is your direct competitor in every market you operate in. And Spring Health has a $3.3 billion valuation. These companies have marketing budgets that dwarf what any independent practice can match dollar-for-dollar.

But here's the strategic insight that changes everything: Talkiatry cannot be local. They can spend more per click, but they can't be the Davie, Florida psychiatrist who takes Aetna, United, and Cigna — who has a Google Business Profile with 200 five-star reviews specific to Davie — who has landing pages that speak to parents in Coral Springs looking for ADHD medication management for their teenager. That is the asymmetric advantage that Refresh Psychiatry holds.

The strategy isn't to outspend the nationals. It's to out-LOCAL them.

Now let's look at Meta advertising — Facebook and Instagram. This is where the landscape is changing fast and Refresh needs to pay attention RIGHT NOW. In January 2025, Meta began rolling out new advertising restrictions for healthcare companies. A second wave of these restrictions is expected in early 2026. Meta is flagging advertisers based on their website content — if you have condition-specific pages about mental health, anxiety, depression, ADHD, you may be automatically classified as a health and wellness advertiser, which triggers additional restrictions on targeting and conversion tracking.

What does this mean practically? It means the "spray and pray" social ad campaigns that worked in 2022 — targeting people by interests like "mental health" or retargeting website visitors — are getting harder. Meta is restricting the use of sensitive health data for targeting. Practices that don't adapt their Meta strategy will see their ad costs climb while their reach shrinks.

For Refresh Psychiatry, with 16 locations, this creates a specific challenge: you need to run location-specific campaigns, but Meta's new restrictions make hyper-local targeting in healthcare more complicated. The workaround is audience building through awareness content rather than direct-to-booking campaigns, paired with strong geographic targeting.

One more channel worth discussing for Refresh specifically: YouTube pre-roll. With a telehealth-first practice, video advertising on YouTube is an underutilized opportunity. A 15-second pre-roll ad featuring Dr. Nepa or a Refresh provider — warm, professional, "we take your insurance, same-week appointments available across Florida, Massachusetts, and Texas" — can reach high-intent healthcare searchers at a fraction of the cost of Google Search. CPC on YouTube is typically $0.25-0.40 compared to $5-7 on Google Search. The volume is lower, but the brand-building value is disproportionate.

SEGMENT 2: THE 16-LOCATION ACCOUNT STRUCTURE — HOW REFRESH SHOULD BUILD ITS GOOGLE ADS CAMPAIGNS

This is where most multi-location practices make a catastrophic mistake: they build one Google Ads campaign and slap geographic radius targeting around their main location. Or worse, they let a generalist agency run one generic "psychiatrist" campaign targeting the entire state of Florida.

For a practice like Refresh Psychiatry — 11 Florida locations, 2 Massachusetts locations, 3 Texas DFW locations — the account structure should be deliberately built around your actual competitive geography. Here's what that looks like in practice.

MARKET CLUSTER APPROACH: Rather than 16 individual campaigns (which becomes unmanageable) or one statewide campaign (which wastes budget and loses precision), Refresh should organize around 5 market clusters:

1. South Florida Metro (Fort Lauderdale/TMW, Davie, Coral Springs, Coconut Creek, Pembroke Pines, Miami) — 6 locations, densest market
2. Palm Beach/Treasure Coast (Wellington, West Palm, Vero Beach) — 3 locations, growing market
3. Central Florida (Orlando, Winter Park) — 2 locations, high competition from Orlando nationals
4. Greater Boston (Boston Seaport, Massachusetts) — 2 locations, premium market, higher CPCs
5. Texas DFW (Dallas, Richardson, Southlake) — 3 locations, rapidly expanding competitive market

Each cluster gets its own campaign budget, its own set of keywords weighted toward that geography, and its own landing pages. The landing page for the South Florida cluster should NOT be the same as the Dallas cluster — patient language, insurance availability (Aetna in FL vs. Texas payers), and even the visual cultural context should differ.

KEYWORD STRATEGY BY INTENT TIER:
The highest-converting keywords in psychiatry are what we call "ready to book" terms — they signal someone who has already decided to get help and is actively choosing a provider. These are:
- "[City] psychiatrist" and "[City] psychiatrist near me"
- "psychiatrist that takes [insurance name]" (HUGE for Refresh — "psychiatrist that takes Aetna Florida," "psychiatrist takes UnitedHealthcare Texas")
- "online psychiatrist Florida," "telehealth psychiatry Massachusetts"
- "ADHD medication management [city]," "anxiety medication online Florida"

The second tier is "condition + solution" searches — someone who has identified their problem and is looking for treatment:
- "ADHD treatment adult Florida," "depression medication online"
- "psychiatrist for anxiety near me," "how to get ADHD diagnosis Florida"

The third tier is broader awareness — "what is telepsychiatry," "online psychiatry." These are cheaper per click but convert at much lower rates. Refresh should limit spend here unless there's a specific brand-building objective.

INSURANCE-SPECIFIC CAMPAIGNS: This is Refresh's single most powerful paid ad differentiator, and most practices miss it completely. Refresh accepts Aetna, United, Cigna, Humana, Avmed, UMR, and Oscar. People DESPERATELY search for in-network providers. The search query "psychiatrist that takes Cigna near me" converts at an extraordinary rate because the person asking it has already overcome the biggest barrier to care — they have insurance and they know they're covered.

Refresh should run dedicated ad groups targeting insurance-specific queries for each major payer. Landing pages for these campaigns should lead with the insurance name prominently: "Refresh Psychiatry — In-Network with Cigna in South Florida. Same-Week Appointments Available." That one sentence answers the two biggest patient questions: can I afford this, and how fast can I be seen?

NEGATIVE KEYWORDS — THE MONEY SAVER: Multi-location practices waste enormous budget on irrelevant clicks. Refresh must build aggressive negative keyword lists including: "jobs," "careers," "salary," "how much does a psychiatrist make," "psychiatry school," "prescribe marijuana," "inpatient," "residential," "crisis hotline," "free therapy," "sliding scale." These negative keywords alone can reduce wasted spend by 20-30%.

CONVERSION TRACKING: This is the difference between a practice that knows its paid advertising is working and one that's just guessing. Refresh must have call tracking set up (a different phone number for each campaign cluster that forwards to the practice) and form submission tracking on every landing page. The goal is to know, for every dollar spent, how many phone calls and form submissions came in — and ideally, how many of those converted to booked appointments. Without this, you're flying blind.

SEGMENT 3: THE REFRESH PSYCHIATRY ACTION PLAN — WHAT TO ACTUALLY DO THIS WEEK

Okay, let's get concrete. If you're Dr. Nepa listening to this on a Sunday morning, here are the specific moves to make this week with your paid advertising.

MOVE 1 — AUDIT YOUR CURRENT GOOGLE ADS ACCOUNT (TODAY, 30 MINUTES)
Log into your Google Ads account and answer these five questions:
1. Are your campaigns organized by geographic cluster or are they one big campaign?
2. Do you have call tracking enabled? Can you see which campaigns are generating phone calls?
3. What is your average CPC across all campaigns? If it's above $8, your account structure needs work.
4. Do you have insurance-specific ad groups? (Aetna, United, Cigna targeting)
5. What is your negative keyword list length? If it's under 50 terms, it's too short.

If you don't have good answers to these questions, you need either a specialized healthcare PPC agency or a serious internal overhaul.

MOVE 2 — BUILD YOUR INSURANCE-SPECIFIC LANDING PAGES (THIS WEEK)
The single highest-ROI improvement Refresh can make right now: create 7 dedicated landing pages — one for each major insurance you accept. Each page should have:
- Headline: "Refresh Psychiatry — In-Network with [Insurance Name]"
- Subheadline: "Same-Week Telehealth Appointments Available in Florida, Massachusetts & Texas"
- Quick insurance verification widget or prominent "call to verify coverage" CTA
- Provider photos and credentials
- Google Reviews widget showing your ratings
- Simple appointment booking form (3 fields: name, phone, insurance)

These pages will dramatically increase your quality score on insurance-related Google queries and drop your CPC by 15-25%.

MOVE 3 — SET UP YOUR META ADVERTISING FOR AWARENESS, NOT DIRECT BOOKING
Given Meta's 2026 healthcare advertising restrictions, shift your Facebook and Instagram strategy from bottom-of-funnel (book appointment now) to top-of-funnel (build awareness and trust). Here's what works:
- Short video content featuring providers: "Hi, I'm Dr. [Name] from Refresh Psychiatry in Davie, FL. Here's what to expect at your first telehealth appointment."
- Educational carousel ads: "5 Signs It's Time to Talk to a Psychiatrist" — no diagnosis implications, just informational
- Geographic targeting by city, not interest-based health targeting (avoids Meta's new restrictions)
- Retarget website visitors using a general "visited refreshpsychiatry.com" audience rather than condition-specific page visits

Budget guidance: For Meta awareness campaigns, $20-40/day per market cluster is sufficient to maintain brand presence. This isn't your primary conversion driver — Google Search is. Meta is your brand visibility and trust-building channel.

MOVE 4 — LAUNCH ONE YOUTUBE PRE-ROLL CAMPAIGN
For under $500/month, Refresh can run YouTube pre-roll ads targeting healthcare-intent viewers in South Florida. Target people watching mental health content, searching for healthcare providers, or within 30 miles of your Davie/Fort Lauderdale locations. A 15-second non-skippable ad featuring a real Refresh provider with a simple CTA: "Same-week appointments. We take your insurance. Book at refreshpsychiatry.com." That's it.

The ROI math is compelling: at $0.30/view and a 1% click-through rate, you're paying $30/click — compared to $5-7 on Google Search. BUT you're also building brand recognition with every impression, even among people who don't click. And in a market where you're competing against Talkiatry's $210M war chest, brand recognition matters.

MOVE 5 — MONTHLY COMPETITIVE AD AUDIT
Use the Google Ads Auction Insights report (available in your Google Ads account for any campaign) to see which competitors are bidding on the same keywords as you. Talkiatry, Cerebral, Done, and local practices will show up here. If Talkiatry is consistently at impression share of 70%+, they're dominating that keyword — consider shifting budget to less competitive terms where Refresh can dominate at lower cost.

Also check Meta Ad Library (facebook.com/ads/library) monthly — search "Talkiatry" and "Cerebral" to see exactly what creative and messaging they're running. This is free competitive intelligence that should inform your own ad creative.

THE BUDGET FRAMEWORK FOR REFRESH: A starting point for a 16-location practice in three states:
- Google Search (primary): $8,000-12,000/month (across all 5 clusters)
- Meta/Instagram (awareness): $2,500-4,000/month
- YouTube pre-roll (brand): $500-1,000/month
- Total: $11,000-17,000/month

At a healthcare CPL of $30-50 on Google and a 30-40% booking conversion rate, that generates 60-100+ new patient appointments per month from paid advertising alone. At an average new patient value of $1,200-2,000+ lifetime (in-network psychiatry with medication management, multiple visits per year), the ROI on well-run paid advertising is 5-10x.
