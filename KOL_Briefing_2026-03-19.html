06 Clinical Insight of the Day
Psilocybin + Psychotherapy Produces Significant, Durable Antidepressant Effects in Treatment-Resistant Depression (EPIsoDE Trial)

Source: EPIsoDE Study, published March 18, 2026 in JAMA Psychiatry. Study Coverage →

Key Finding: In this Phase 3 randomized controlled trial, psilocybin combined with psychotherapy demonstrated statistically significant reduction in depression severity at 6 weeks in patients with treatment-resistant depression, with effects shown to be long-lasting. No new safety signals were identified. This is the largest controlled psilocybin trial for TRD to date and adds to the COMP360 Phase 3 data from Compass Pathways.

Clinical Pearl: For patients with TRD who have failed 2+ adequate antidepressant trials, the evidence base for psychedelic-assisted therapy is now reaching a threshold that warrants discussion in treatment planning conversations — even before regulatory approval. Clinicians should understand the mechanistic rationale (5-HT2A agonism promoting neuroplasticity), the requirement for structured psychotherapeutic support, and the current regulatory landscape (still investigational in the US, legal in Oregon and Colorado under state frameworks).

Three Ways to Use This Insight

1. As a Presentation Talking Point

"The EPIsoDE trial published this week in JAMA Psychiatry represents a potential inflection point for TRD treatment. For the first time, we have Phase 3 evidence that psilocybin combined with structured psychotherapy produces significant, durable antidepressant effects. As integrative psychiatrists, we need to be prepared to discuss these emerging options with patients — and to understand both the promise and the limitations."

2. As a LinkedIn Post / Short Video Script

"Breaking: JAMA Psychiatry just published the EPIsoDE trial — the largest controlled study of psilocybin for treatment-resistant depression. The results? Significant, lasting improvement in depression severity at 6 weeks. 3. As a CME Lecture / Abstract Foundation
