# ADHD Treatment in Massachusetts -- Expert Psychiatric Care Across the Commonwealth

## Understanding ADHD: More Than Just Difficulty Focusing

Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder affects thousands of adults and children across Massachusetts. Whether you are a college student in Boston struggling to keep up with coursework, a working professional in Worcester finding it harder to manage daily responsibilities, or a parent in Springfield noticing your child falling behind in school, ADHD is a real and treatable condition.

ADHD is not a matter of willpower or intelligence. It is a neurological condition that affects executive function, attention regulation, impulse control, and working memory. Without proper diagnosis and treatment, ADHD can lead to difficulties in work, relationships, academics, and overall quality of life.

At Mass Psychiatry, we provide comprehensive, evidence-based ADHD evaluations and treatment plans tailored to your individual needs -- all through convenient telepsychiatry appointments.

## Signs and Symptoms of ADHD

ADHD presents differently in every person. Common symptoms include:

- Difficulty sustaining attention during tasks or conversations
- Frequently losing important items such as keys, phones, or documents
- Trouble organizing tasks, managing time, and meeting deadlines
- Restlessness, fidgeting, or feeling driven by an internal motor
- Impulsive decision-making or difficulty waiting your turn
- Chronic procrastination despite wanting to complete tasks
- Emotional dysregulation, including frustration and mood swings

In adults, ADHD is often underdiagnosed because symptoms may look different than the hyperactivity commonly associated with childhood ADHD. Many adults describe feeling scattered, overwhelmed, or chronically underperforming despite their best efforts.

- **Ongoing follow-up appointments** to monitor progress, adjust treatment, and address any concerns
- **Collaborative care planning** that may include recommendations for therapy, coaching, or lifestyle modifications

We believe in treating the whole person, not just a checklist of symptoms. Your treatment plan will reflect your unique goals, lifestyle, and medical history.

# Depression Treatment in Massachusetts -- Professional Psychiatric Support When You Need It Most

## You Are Not Alone in This

Depression is one of the most common and most treatable mental health conditions, yet many people in Massachusetts go months or even years without receiving proper care. Whether you are in Boston, Worcester, New Bedford, or a small town in western Massachusetts, depression can make even the simplest daily tasks feel overwhelming.

At Mass Psychiatry, we understand how isolating depression can feel. That is why we offer telepsychiatry services across the entire Commonwealth -- bringing expert psychiatric care directly to you through secure video appointments.

## What Depression Looks Like

Depression is more than sadness. It is a medical condition that affects how you think, feel, and function. Symptoms may include:

- Persistent low mood or feelings of emptiness lasting two weeks or longer
- Loss of interest or pleasure in activities you once enjoyed
- Changes in appetite or weight -- either significant increase or decrease
- Sleep disturbances, including insomnia or sleeping excessively
- Fatigue and loss of energy, even after rest
- Difficulty concentrating, making decisions, or remembering things
- Feelings of worthlessness, guilt, or hopelessness
- Withdrawal from friends, family, and social activities
- In severe cases, thoughts of death or suicide

If you are experiencing thoughts of self-harm or suicide, please call 988 (Suicide and Crisis Lifeline) or go to your nearest emergency room immediately.

## Our Approach to Treating Depression

Mass Psychiatry provides personalized, evidence-based treatment for all forms of depression, including:

- **Major Depressive Disorder** -- single or recurrent episodes of significant depression
- **Persistent Depressive Disorder (Dysthymia)** -- chronic, lower-grade depression lasting two or more years
- **Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD)** -- depression related to seasonal changes, particularly common during long Massachusetts winters
- **Postpartum Depression** -- depression occurring during pregnancy or after childbirth
- **Treatment-Resistant Depression** -- cases where initial treatments have not provided adequate relief

Our treatment process includes a comprehensive psychiatric evaluation, medication management with careful selection and monitoring, and coordination with your therapist or primary care provider when appropriate.

## Medications We May Prescribe

Depending on your diagnosis and individual needs, treatment options may include:

- SSRIs (such as sertraline, escitalopram, or fluoxetine)
- SNRIs (such as venlafaxine or duloxetine)

- Augmentation strategies for treatment-resistant cases

Every medication decision is made collaboratively. We explain your options, discuss potential benefits and side effects, and respect your preferences throughout the process.

# Bipolar Disorder Treatment in Massachusetts -- Specialized Psychiatric Care You Can Trust

## Understanding Bipolar Disorder

Bipolar disorder is a complex mood condition characterized by significant shifts in energy, activity levels, and emotional states. These shifts go far beyond normal ups and downs -- they can profoundly affect your ability to function at work, maintain relationships, and take care of daily responsibilities.

Bipolar disorder affects people in every community across Massachusetts, from busy urban centers like Boston and Worcester to quieter towns on Cape Cod and throughout the Berkshires. Despite its prevalence, bipolar disorder is frequently misdiagnosed -- often confused with depression, anxiety, or ADHD. Accurate diagnosis is essential, because the wrong treatment can actually make symptoms worse.

Mass Psychiatry provides expert bipolar disorder evaluation and ongoing medication management through telepsychiatry, giving you access to specialized care from anywhere in the Commonwealth.

## Types of Bipolar Disorder

We diagnose and treat all forms of bipolar disorder:

- **Bipolar I Disorder** -- defined by manic episodes lasting at least seven days (or requiring hospitalization), often alternating with depressive episodes
- **Bipolar II Disorder** -- characterized by hypomanic episodes (less severe than full mania) and significant depressive episodes
- **Cyclothymic Disorder** -- chronic, fluctuating mood disturbances with periods of hypomania and mild depression lasting at least two years
- **Other Specified Bipolar Disorder** -- presentations that do not fit neatly into the above categories but still require treatment

## Recognizing the Signs

Bipolar disorder symptoms vary depending on whether a person is experiencing a manic, hypomanic, or depressive episode:

**During manic or hypomanic episodes:**
- Unusually elevated, expansive, or irritable mood
- Decreased need for sleep without feeling tired
- Racing thoughts and rapid speech
- Increased goal-directed activity or physical agitation
- Impulsive behavior including excessive spending, risky decisions, or uncharacteristic social behavior
- Inflated self-confidence or grandiosity

**During depressive episodes:**
- Persistent sadness, hopelessness, or emptiness
- Loss of interest in previously enjoyable activities
- Fatigue and difficulty getting through the day
- Concentration problems and indecisiveness
- Changes in sleep and appetite

## How We Treat Bipolar Disorder

Effective bipolar disorder management requires specialized psychiatric expertise and consistent follow-up. Our approach includes:

- **Detailed diagnostic assessment** using clinical interviews and validated tools to distinguish bipolar disorder from other conditions
- **Mood stabilizer management** including lithium, valproate, lamotrigine, and other evidence-based options
- **Atypical antipsychotic medications** when indicated for acute episodes or maintenance treatment
- **Careful avoidance of treatments that may destabilize mood**, such as antidepressant monotherapy
- **Regular monitoring** of symptoms, medication levels, and side effects
- **Crisis planning and safety assessment** to prepare for potential mood episodes

- **Psychiatric providers with specific expertise** in mood disorders and bipolar spectrum conditions
- **Telepsychiatry access across Massachusetts** -- from Springfield to Boston to New Bedford and beyond
- **Consistent, ongoing care** that is critical for bipolar disorder management
- **Medication monitoring** with attention to lab work, side effects, and long-term health
- **Collaborative approach** that includes coordination with therapists and other members of your care team

## Get the Right Diagnosis and the Right Treatment

If you suspect you may have bipolar disorder, or if a previous treatment for depression or anxiety has not worked as expected, a thorough psychiatric evaluation can make all the difference.

# OCD Treatment in Massachusetts -- Expert Psychiatric Care for Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder

## What Is OCD, Really?

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder is widely misunderstood. It is not about being neat or organized. OCD is a serious mental health condition in which a person experiences intrusive, unwanted thoughts (obsessions) and feels compelled to perform repetitive behaviors or mental acts (compulsions) to reduce the distress those thoughts cause.

OCD affects approximately 2-3% of the population, and people across Massachusetts -- in Boston, Cambridge, Lowell, Worcester, the South Shore, and beyond -- live with its effects every day. Without proper treatment, OCD can consume hours of a person's day and severely limit their ability to work, study, socialize, and enjoy life.

Mass Psychiatry offers specialized psychiatric evaluation and medication management for OCD through convenient virtual appointments across the Commonwealth.

## Common OCD Presentations

OCD can take many forms. Some of the most common presentations include:

- **Contamination fears** -- excessive worry about germs, illness, or contamination leading to repeated handwashing, cleaning, or avoidance
- **Harm obsessions** -- intrusive, unwanted thoughts about causing harm to oneself or others, despite having no desire to do so
- **Symmetry and ordering** -- a need for things to be arranged in a specific way, often accompanied by distress when they are not
- **Checking compulsions** -- repeatedly verifying that doors are locked, appliances are off, or tasks have been completed correctly
- **Religious or moral obsessions (scrupulosity)** -- excessive concern about offending God, committing sins, or violating moral principles
- **"Just right" feelings** -- a persistent sense that something is not right until a compulsion is performed
- **Pure O (primarily obsessional OCD)** -- intrusive thoughts with mental rather than visible compulsions

## How We Treat OCD at Mass Psychiatry

The gold standard for OCD treatment combines medication management with Exposure and Response Prevention (ERP) therapy. At Mass Psychiatry, we provide the psychiatric medication component and coordinate closely with therapists who specialize in ERP.

Our medication management approach includes:

- **SSRIs at therapeutic doses for OCD** -- OCD often requires higher doses than those used for depression or anxiety
- **Clomipramine** when SSRIs are insufficient
- **Augmentation strategies** including low-dose atypical antipsychotics for treatment-resistant cases
- **Careful monitoring** with regular follow-up to track symptom improvement and manage side effects
- **Coordination with your therapist** to ensure medication and therapy work together effectively

## Why Choose Mass Psychiatry for OCD Treatment

- **Providers who understand OCD** and its many presentations -- not just the stereotypes
- **Virtual care from anywhere in Massachusetts** including Boston, Springfield, Fall River, Brockton, and the Cape
- **Evidence-based medication management** using protocols supported by current research
- **No judgment** -- OCD thoughts can feel deeply distressing and shameful, and we provide a safe space to discuss them
- **Collaborative treatment planning** that respects your goals and preferences

## Start Getting the Help You Deserve

OCD thrives on secrecy and avoidance. Reaching out for help is one of the most important steps you can take. With proper treatment, most people with OCD experience meaningful reduction in symptoms and significant improvements in their daily lives.

# Mood Disorder Treatment in Massachusetts -- Comprehensive Psychiatric Care for Emotional Balance

## When Your Mood Feels Out of Your Control

Mood disorders encompass a broad range of conditions in which persistent changes in emotional state significantly affect your daily life. If you find that your moods are unpredictable, intense, or lasting far longer than expected, you may be dealing with a diagnosable and treatable mood disorder.

Across Massachusetts -- from the bustling streets of Boston and Cambridge to communities in the Pioneer Valley, MetroWest, and the South Coast -- thousands of residents live with mood disorders that go undiagnosed or undertreated. Mass Psychiatry is here to change that with accessible, expert telepsychiatry services available to anyone in the Commonwealth.

## Conditions Under the Mood Disorder Umbrella

Mood disorders include several distinct diagnoses, each requiring a specific treatment approach:

- **Major Depressive Disorder** -- episodes of pervasive low mood, loss of interest, and functional impairment
- **Persistent Depressive Disorder (Dysthymia)** -- chronic, lower-intensity depression lasting two or more years
- **Bipolar I and Bipolar II Disorder** -- mood episodes that alternate between depression and mania or hypomania
- **Cyclothymic Disorder** -- chronic mood fluctuations that are less severe but still disruptive
- **Premenstrual Dysphoric Disorder (PMDD)** -- severe mood disturbance tied to the menstrual cycle
- **Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD)** -- mood changes connected to seasonal patterns, especially during Massachusetts winters when daylight hours are limited
- **Disruptive Mood Dysregulation Disorder** -- chronic irritability and anger outbursts in children and adolescents
- **Substance-Induced or Medical Mood Disorders** -- mood symptoms caused by medications, substances, or medical conditions

## How We Diagnose Mood Disorders

Accurate diagnosis is the foundation of effective treatment. Our evaluation process includes:

- A detailed clinical interview covering your symptoms, history, and family background
- Assessment of symptom patterns, triggers, duration, and severity
- Screening for co-occurring conditions such as anxiety, ADHD, or substance use
- Review of any prior treatments and their outcomes
- Consideration of medical factors that may be contributing to mood symptoms

We take the time to get the full picture before making a diagnosis or recommending treatment.

## Our Treatment Approach

Treatment is tailored to your specific diagnosis and individual needs. Options may include:

- **Antidepressant medications** for depressive disorders
- **Mood stabilizers** for bipolar spectrum conditions
- **Hormonal or targeted treatments** for PMDD
- **Light therapy recommendations** for seasonal affective disorder
- **Medication combinations or augmentation** for complex or treatment-resistant presentations
- **Therapy referrals** to complement medication management

We monitor your progress closely with regular follow-up appointments and adjust your treatment plan as needed.

# Insomnia Treatment in Massachusetts -- Psychiatric Care for Better Sleep and Better Health

## The Hidden Cost of Sleepless Nights

Chronic insomnia is far more than an inconvenience. When you consistently struggle to fall asleep, stay asleep, or wake up feeling rested, it affects every aspect of your life -- your energy, your mood, your concentration, your relationships, and your physical health.

An estimated one in three adults experiences insomnia symptoms, and many residents across Massachusetts -- from high-pressure professionals in Boston and Cambridge to shift workers in Worcester and Springfield -- deal with persistent sleep problems without ever receiving proper evaluation or treatment.

At Mass Psychiatry, we treat insomnia as the serious medical concern it is. Through virtual appointments available across the entire Commonwealth, we help you identify the root causes of your sleep difficulties and develop an effective, sustainable treatment plan.

## Types of Insomnia We Treat

- **Chronic Insomnia Disorder** -- difficulty sleeping at least three nights per week for three months or longer
- **Short-Term Insomnia** -- sleep difficulties related to identifiable stressors, life changes, or medical events
- **Insomnia Co-Occurring with Mental Health Conditions** -- sleep disruption connected to anxiety, depression, PTSD, bipolar disorder, or other psychiatric conditions
- **Medication-Related Sleep Disturbance** -- insomnia caused or worsened by prescription medications or substances

## Common Insomnia Symptoms

You may benefit from an evaluation if you regularly experience:

- Difficulty falling asleep despite feeling tired
- Waking up multiple times during the night
- Waking up too early and being unable to return to sleep
- Daytime fatigue, irritability, or difficulty concentrating
- Anxiety or dread about going to bed
- Reliance on alcohol, over-the-counter sleep aids, or other substances to fall asleep
- Feeling unrefreshed in the morning regardless of time spent in bed

## Our Approach to Insomnia Treatment

Mass Psychiatry takes a comprehensive approach to sleep problems. Rather than simply prescribing a sleeping pill, we work to understand the underlying factors driving your insomnia.

Our treatment strategies include:

- **Assessment of co-occurring psychiatric conditions** that may be causing or worsening insomnia, such as anxiety, depression, or PTSD
- **Sleep hygiene education and behavioral strategies** as a foundation for better sleep
- **Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia (CBT-I) referrals** -- the first-line treatment recommended by the American Academy of Sleep Medicine
- **Medication management when appropriate**, including non-habit-forming options such as low-dose trazodone, hydroxyzine, or melatonin receptor agonists
- **Review and adjustment of current medications** that may be interfering with sleep
- **Gradual tapering plans** for patients who want to reduce dependence on benzodiazepines or Z-drugs

## The Connection Between Sleep and Mental Health

Insomnia and psychiatric conditions often reinforce each other in a cycle. Poor sleep worsens anxiety and depression, and untreated anxiety and depression make it harder to sleep. Addressing both sides of this relationship is essential for lasting improvement -- and it is something our psychiatric providers are uniquely qualified to do.

- **Virtual appointments** from the comfort of your home, available across Massachusetts -- Boston, Lowell, Fall River, Cape Cod, the Berkshires, and everywhere in between
- **A thorough approach** that looks beyond the surface to find the real cause of your sleep problems
- **Non-addictive treatment options** prioritized whenever possible

You should not have to accept poor sleep as your normal. Effective insomnia treatment can improve not only your nights but your entire quality of life.
