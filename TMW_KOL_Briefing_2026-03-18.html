Section 6: Clinical Insight of the Day

Psilocybin in Treatment-Resistant Depression: What Two Phase 3 Wins Mean for Clinical Practice

The Evidence: Compass Pathways' COMP360 psilocybin (25 mg) achieved its primary endpoint in both Phase 3 trials (COMP005 and COMP006), demonstrating statistically significant and clinically meaningful reductions in MADRS scores. The effect was rapid (significant from Day 1 post-dose) and durable — participants with clinically meaningful responses in COMP005 maintained benefit through at least Week 26 after just 1–2 doses.

The Clinical Pearl: Unlike traditional antidepressants requiring daily dosing, psilocybin's therapeutic model involves 1–2 administered sessions with therapeutic support. This challenges the chronic daily medication paradigm. However, important nuances exist: (1) treatment requires specialized training and supervised administration in certified settings, (2) the FDA will likely require a REMS program, and (3) patient selection criteria remain critical — not all TRD patients are candidates. Importantly, the FDA also accepted Compass's IND for COMP360 in PTSD, suggesting the mechanism may extend beyond depression.

Source: Compass Pathways investor release, March 2026; Compass Pathways Press Release
