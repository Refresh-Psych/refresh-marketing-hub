### Understanding Bipolar Disorder

Bipolar disorder is a complex mood condition characterized by significant shifts in energy, activity levels, and emotional states. These shifts -- which include episodes of mania or hypomania and periods of depression -- go well beyond ordinary mood changes. Without proper treatment, bipolar disorder can disrupt careers, relationships, finances, and overall quality of life.

Lonestar Psychiatry provides specialized bipolar disorder evaluation and treatment to patients across the Dallas-Fort Worth area and throughout Texas. Our providers bring the clinical expertise needed to help you achieve stability and build a life that feels sustainable.

### Types of Bipolar Disorder

Bipolar disorder exists on a spectrum. The most common forms include:

- **Bipolar I Disorder** -- Defined by manic episodes lasting at least seven days, often accompanied by depressive episodes
- **Bipolar II Disorder** -- Characterized by hypomanic episodes (less severe than full mania) alternating with major depressive episodes
- **Cyclothymic Disorder** -- Chronic, fluctuating mood disturbances involving periods of hypomanic and depressive symptoms that do not meet full diagnostic criteria

Accurate diagnosis is essential because bipolar disorder is frequently misdiagnosed as depression alone. When only the depressive side is treated, outcomes can worsen. A comprehensive psychiatric evaluation at Lonestar Psychiatry ensures the full clinical picture is considered.

### Our Treatment Approach

Managing bipolar disorder effectively requires ongoing psychiatric care and careful medication management. At Lonestar Psychiatry, your treatment plan is built around your specific diagnosis, symptoms, and life circumstances. Treatment may include:

- **Mood stabilizers** -- Lithium, valproate, lamotrigine, and other medications proven to reduce the frequency and severity of mood episodes
- **Atypical antipsychotics** -- Used alone or in combination to manage manic, mixed, or depressive episodes
- **Careful antidepressant use** -- When indicated, prescribed alongside mood stabilizers to minimize the risk of triggering mania
- **Sleep and routine optimization** -- Establishing consistent daily rhythms that support mood stability
- **Regular monitoring** -- Frequent follow-up visits to track progress, adjust medications, and address emerging concerns early

We know that bipolar disorder management is a long-term partnership, and we are committed to being a reliable part of your care team.

### What Is Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder?

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder is a mental health condition marked by intrusive, unwanted thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive behaviors or mental acts (compulsions) performed to relieve the distress those thoughts cause. OCD is not about being neat or particular -- it is a serious condition that can consume hours of a person's day and cause significant emotional suffering.

Lonestar Psychiatry provides psychiatric evaluation and medication management for OCD to patients across the Dallas-Fort Worth metroplex and throughout Texas. If OCD is disrupting your daily life, we are here to help.

### Recognizing the Signs of OCD

OCD manifests in many forms. Common obsessions and compulsions include:

- **Contamination fears** -- Excessive worry about germs, illness, or environmental toxins, leading to repeated handwashing or cleaning rituals
- **Harm obsessions** -- Intrusive thoughts about accidentally or intentionally hurting yourself or others
- **Symmetry and ordering** -- A need for things to be arranged in a specific way, with distress when they are not
- **Checking compulsions** -- Repeatedly verifying that doors are locked, appliances are off, or tasks are completed
- **Mental rituals** -- Counting, praying, or repeating phrases silently to neutralize distressing thoughts
- **"Just right" feelings** -- A persistent sense that something is not right until a specific action is performed

Many people with OCD feel ashamed of their symptoms and delay seeking help. It is important to know that OCD is a neurobiological condition, not a character flaw, and effective treatments exist.

### Evidence-Based OCD Treatment

At Lonestar Psychiatry, we use proven psychiatric approaches to help reduce the intensity and frequency of OCD symptoms. Your personalized treatment plan may include:

- **SSRI medication** -- Selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors are the first-line pharmacological treatment for OCD, often at higher doses than those used for depression
- **Medication augmentation** -- For patients who do not fully respond to SSRIs, additional medications may be added to enhance effectiveness
- **Coordination with therapy** -- OCD responds best to a combination of medication and Exposure and Response Prevention (ERP) therapy. We work collaboratively with your therapist to ensure integrated care
- **Ongoing monitoring and adjustment** -- OCD treatment often requires patience and fine-tuning. We provide consistent follow-up to optimize your results

### What Are Mood Disorders?

Mood disorders are a category of mental health conditions in which persistent disturbances in emotional state significantly affect your ability to function. Unlike temporary sadness or stress, mood disorders involve changes in mood that last for weeks, months, or longer and interfere with work, relationships, and daily life.

Lonestar Psychiatry provides expert evaluation and treatment for the full spectrum of mood disorders, serving patients across Dallas, Fort Worth, Arlington, Plano, Frisco, McKinney, Irving, and all Texas communities through convenient telepsychiatry.

### Conditions Under the Mood Disorder Umbrella

Mood disorders encompass a range of diagnoses, including:

- **Major Depressive Disorder** -- Persistent depressed mood, loss of interest, and functional impairment
- **Persistent Depressive Disorder (Dysthymia)** -- A chronic, lower-grade depression lasting two years or more
- **Bipolar I and Bipolar II Disorder** -- Mood episodes that alternate between depression and mania or hypomania
- **Cyclothymic Disorder** -- Chronic mood fluctuations that do not meet full criteria for bipolar disorder or major depression
- **Premenstrual Dysphoric Disorder (PMDD)** -- Severe mood symptoms tied to the menstrual cycle
- **Seasonal Affective Disorder** -- Depressive episodes that follow a seasonal pattern
- **Disruptive Mood Dysregulation Disorder** -- Severe irritability and frequent outbursts in children and adolescents

Because mood disorders often overlap or co-occur with anxiety, ADHD, trauma-related conditions, and substance use, accurate diagnosis is the foundation of effective treatment.

### Our Comprehensive Approach

At Lonestar Psychiatry, we begin every treatment relationship with a thorough diagnostic evaluation. We take the time to understand your history, symptoms, and goals before making any recommendations. Your treatment plan may include:

- **Targeted medication management** -- Antidepressants, mood stabilizers, atypical antipsychotics, or combination strategies selected for your specific diagnosis

- **Regular reassessment** -- Mood disorders can shift over time, and your treatment plan should evolve alongside your needs
- **Integrated care coordination** -- Partnering with therapists, primary care physicians, and other providers to ensure a unified approach

We believe that accurate diagnosis and thoughtful medication management can transform outcomes for people living with mood disorders.

### When Sleep Becomes a Struggle

Sleep is fundamental to mental and physical health, yet millions of adults across Texas struggle with insomnia. If you spend your nights staring at the ceiling, waking repeatedly, or rising exhausted despite spending enough time in bed, you understand how profoundly poor sleep affects every part of your life -- your mood, your focus, your patience, and your overall well-being.

Lonestar Psychiatry offers specialized psychiatric care for insomnia and sleep-related concerns to patients throughout the Dallas-Fort Worth area and across the state of Texas.

### Understanding Insomnia

Insomnia is more than an occasional restless night. Clinical insomnia involves:

- Difficulty falling asleep despite adequate opportunity
- Waking frequently during the night and struggling to return to sleep
- Waking too early in the morning without being able to fall back asleep
- Daytime impairment such as fatigue, irritability, poor concentration, or low motivation
- Symptoms persisting for at least three months (chronic insomnia)

Insomnia frequently co-occurs with other mental health conditions, including anxiety, depression, PTSD, and bipolar disorder. In many cases, treating insomnia improves these conditions as well -- and vice versa. A psychiatric evaluation can identify whether your sleep difficulties are a primary condition or part of a broader clinical picture.

### Our Approach to Insomnia Treatment

At Lonestar Psychiatry, we take a comprehensive view of sleep health. Rather than simply prescribing a sleeping pill, we investigate the underlying causes of your insomnia and create a treatment plan that addresses the full picture. Your plan may include:

- **Sleep hygiene optimization** -- Practical, evidence-based adjustments to your sleep environment, routines, and habits
- **Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia (CBT-I) referrals** -- CBT-I is the gold-standard treatment for chronic insomnia, and we coordinate with therapists trained in this approach
- **Medication management** -- When medication is appropriate, we use options with favorable safety profiles, carefully weighing benefits and risks
- **Treatment of co-occurring conditions** -- Addressing anxiety, depression, or other conditions that may be driving or worsening your insomnia
- **Gradual deprescribing** -- For patients who want to reduce reliance on sleep medications, we provide a safe, structured tapering plan
