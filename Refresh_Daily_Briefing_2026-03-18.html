3. 🧪 Emerging Off-Label Treatments in Psychiatry

⚠️ Rotating Section — Today: 3 novel off-label uses you may not have considered.

1️⃣ Solriamfetol (Sunosi) — Off-Label for Treatment-Resistant Depression

FieldDetail
Original IndicationExcessive daytime sleepiness in narcolepsy/OSA
Off-Label UseAdjunctive treatment for MDD, particularly with fatigue/anhedonia predominance
MechanismDopamine & norepinephrine reuptake inhibitor + TAAR1 agonist — dual mechanism provides both alerting and mood-elevating effects
EvidencePhase 3 trials for depression initiated 2024; open-label data promising. Psychiatric Times Pipeline Review
Patient PopulationAdults with MDD and prominent fatigue, hypersomnia, or anhedonia who've failed ≥2 SSRIs/SNRIs
RisksInsomnia, headache, nausea, decreased appetite, potential BP elevation. Not a controlled substance but monitor carefully.
Telehealth FeasibilityHIGH — Oral medication, no in-person requirements. Standard vitals monitoring.
Refresh Relevance✅ Worth exploring. Many TRD patients present with fatigue as chief complaint. Could differentiate Refresh's offering.

2️⃣ Negative Allosteric Modulators of mGlu7 — Anxiety & PTSD

FieldDetail
Drug ClassMetabotropic glutamate receptor 7 (mGlu7) negative allosteric modulators
Off-Label UseAnxiety disorders and PTSD — targeting glutamatergic dysregulation
MechanismBlocks mGlu7 autoreceptors, enhancing glutamate release at specific synapses involved in fear extinction and emotional regulation
EvidencePreclinical and early-phase data published 2025-2026. Neuropsychopharmacology 2026
Patient PopulationPTSD/anxiety patients who've failed SSRIs and benzodiazepines
RisksEarly stage — safety profile not fully established. Glutamate modulation carries theoretical seizure risk.
Telehealth FeasibilityMODERATE — Not yet available clinically. Monitor for Phase 2 trials.
Refresh Relevance📌 Watch list only. Important paradigm shift from GABA to glutamate in anxiety treatment. Brief patients on emerging science.

3️⃣ Pomaglumetad Methionil (DB103) — First-Episode Psychosis

FieldDetail
Original DevelopmentOriginally developed by Eli Lilly; failed Phase 3 for schizophrenia (broad population). Now being repositioned by Denovo Biopharma using biomarker-guided patient selection.
Off-Label UseFirst-episode psychosis and clinical high risk for psychosis — a precision psychiatry approach
MechanismFirst-in-class mGlu2/3 receptor agonist — modulates glutamate without direct dopamine blockade, avoiding metabolic and EPS side effects
EvidencePhase 2 trial planned in partnership with Orygen (Australia). Biomarker-selected subpopulations showed robust response in retrospective analyses.
Patient PopulationYoung adults (18-30) experiencing first psychotic episode or prodromal symptoms
RisksPreviously failed in unselected populations; biomarker testing required. Limited safety data in new target population.
Telehealth FeasibilityMODERATE — First-episode psychosis typically needs in-person initial assessment.
Refresh Relevance📌 Long-term watch. If biomarker-guided approach succeeds, this could fundamentally change early psychosis intervention.
