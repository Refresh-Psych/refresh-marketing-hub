Key Findings & Patterns

  • Concentration of payments: The top 2.8% of psychiatrists received 82.6% of all pharma payments to the specialty.
  • Total industry spend: $340 million to U.S. psychiatrists from 2014-2020 ($212M for speaking, $33M consulting, remainder for travel/food/beverages).
  • Seven key drugs: Abilify Maintena, Brintellix/Trintellix, Rexulti, Vraylar, Aristada, Austedo, and Ingrezza accounted for the lion's share of promotional funding.
  • Top paying companies: Otsuka ($20.4M), Alkermes ($14.9M), Sunovion ($14.8M), Allergan ($13.9M), Takeda ($10.6M) -- these 5 companies = ~68% of all psychiatrist payments.
  • ASCP leadership: ASCP officers collectively received $4.62M; board members $3.37M (total $8M 2014-2020).
  • Per-event fees: Standard speakers earn $1,500-$3,000/talk; top KOLs earn $3,500-$7,500; elite academic KOLs earn $5,000-$15,000+ per engagement.
  • Volume vs. rate: Some KOLs (Jain, Roma, Bojrab) maximize earnings through extreme volume (200-300+ talks/year at $2,000-$3,000 each), while others (Stahl, Nemeroff, Kane) command higher per-event fees but speak less frequently.
  • 75% of all U.S. psychiatrists (31,784) received something of value from pharma during this period.
