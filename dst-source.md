# Daylight Saving Time and Mental Health: Key Facts for Refresh Psychiatry Blog

## SLEEP DISRUPTION
- Losing 1 hour of sleep disrupts circadian rhythm for 5-7 days
- The average person loses 40 minutes of sleep the week after spring forward
- Sleep quality decreases significantly, not just duration
- REM sleep is disproportionately affected, impacting emotional regulation

## MOOD AND MENTAL HEALTH IMPACTS
- Studies show an 11% increase in depressive episodes following spring DST transition
- Anxiety symptoms increase 15-20% in the week following the time change
- Irritability and difficulty concentrating are commonly reported
- Existing mental health conditions (depression, bipolar, anxiety disorders) may worsen
- Serotonin and melatonin production are disrupted by the light schedule change

## PHYSICAL SYMPTOMS
- 24% increase in heart attacks the Monday after spring forward (University of Michigan study)
- Increased cortisol (stress hormone) levels
- Appetite changes and digestive disruption
- Headaches and fatigue lasting up to a week
- Decreased immune function

## VULNERABLE POPULATIONS
- People with existing mood disorders
- Those on psychiatric medications (timing sensitivity)
- Parents of young children
- Shift workers
- Older adults

## EVIDENCE-BASED COPING STRATEGIES
- Gradually adjust bedtime 15 minutes earlier for 4 days before the change
- Get morning sunlight exposure within 30 minutes of waking
- Avoid caffeine after 2 PM during the adjustment week
- Maintain consistent meal times
- Exercise in the morning rather than evening
- Practice good sleep hygiene: dark room, cool temperature, no screens 1 hour before bed
- Be gentle with yourself — it is a real physiological adjustment
- Contact your mental health provider if symptoms persist beyond 2 weeks

## KEY STATISTICS
- 63% of Americans report feeling tired after DST
- Workplace injuries increase 5.7% the Monday after spring forward
- Fatal car accidents increase 6% in the week following spring forward
- The American Academy of Sleep Medicine supports eliminating DST changes
- The spring transition is harder than fall because losing sleep is more disruptive than gaining it

## WHY THIS MATTERS FOR PSYCHIATRIC PATIENTS
- Medication timing may need temporary adjustment (consult your provider)
- Light therapy timing shifts with DST
- Mood tracking is especially important during transition weeks
- Sleep disruption is a known trigger for mood episodes in bipolar disorder
- Anxiety and panic attacks may increase due to cortisol dysregulation
