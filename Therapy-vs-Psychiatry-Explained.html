Therapy vs. Psychiatry Explained: Understanding Your Mental Health Care Options

What Is Therapy (Psychotherapy)?

Therapy, also known as psychotherapy or counseling, is a treatment approach that uses conversation-based techniques to help you understand and work through emotional challenges, behavioral patterns, and mental health conditions.

  • Providers: Licensed therapists (LCSW, LMHC, LMFT), psychologists (PhD, PsyD)
  • Methods: Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT), EMDR, psychodynamic therapy, motivational interviewing
  • Focus: Developing coping skills, processing trauma, changing thought patterns, improving relationships, building emotional resilience
  • Session format: Typically 45-60 minute weekly sessions
  • No prescribing authority: Therapists cannot prescribe medication (in most states)

Best for: Anxiety, depression, relationship issues, grief, trauma/PTSD, life transitions, stress management, personal growth

What Is Psychiatry?

Psychiatry is a medical specialty focused on diagnosing, treating, and preventing mental health disorders. Psychiatrists are medical doctors (MD or DO) who completed medical school and specialized residency training in mental health.

  • Providers: Psychiatrists (MD, DO), Psychiatric Nurse Practitioners (PMHNP)
  • Methods: Medication management, diagnostic evaluation, treatment planning, Spravato (esketamine), TMS, genetic testing for medication optimization
  • Focus: Medical evaluation of symptoms, neurotransmitter-based treatment, medication prescribing and monitoring, complex diagnostic assessment
  • Session format: Initial evaluation 60-90 minutes, follow-up medication checks 15-30 minutes
  • Prescribing authority: Can prescribe and manage psychiatric medications including antidepressants, mood stabilizers, antipsychotics, stimulants, and anti-anxiety medications

Best for: ADHD, major depression, bipolar disorder, schizophrenia, OCD, severe anxiety, medication-resistant conditions, complex multi-diagnosis cases

When You Might Need Both

Research consistently shows that the combination of therapy and medication management produces the best outcomes for many conditions. The American Psychiatric Association recommends combined treatment for moderate to severe depression, anxiety disorders, PTSD, and OCD.

  • Depression: Medication can stabilize brain chemistry while therapy addresses thought patterns and coping skills
  • ADHD: Stimulant medication improves focus while therapy builds organizational strategies and self-regulation
  • Anxiety: Anti-anxiety or SSRI medication reduces acute symptoms while CBT provides long-term tools
  • Bipolar disorder: Mood stabilizers are essential; therapy helps with lifestyle management and early warning signs
  • PTSD: Medication manages symptoms like insomnia and hyperarousal; EMDR or CPT processes traumatic memories

How to Decide What's Right for You

Start with therapy if: You're dealing with life stress, relationship issues, mild to moderate anxiety or depression, grief, or want to develop better coping skills.

Start with psychiatry if: You're experiencing severe symptoms affecting daily function, have tried therapy without sufficient improvement, suspect you may have ADHD or bipolar disorder, or need a comprehensive diagnostic evaluation.

Consider both if: You have moderate to severe symptoms, a complex diagnosis, or want the most comprehensive treatment approach.
