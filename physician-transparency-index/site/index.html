About This Data

Where does this data come from?

All data comes from the CMS Open Payments database, a federal program that collects and publishes information about payments pharmaceutical and medical device companies make to physicians.

Does a high payment total mean a doctor is corrupt?

No. Many legitimate activities generate significant industry payments, including clinical research, medical education, advisory boards, and consulting. This tool promotes transparency, not accusations.

How is the risk score calculated?

The risk score is an algorithmic estimate based on payment volume, company concentration, payment consistency across years, outlier status within specialty, and payment type mix. It ranges from 0-100.

How is this different from Dollars for Docs?

Dollars for Docs by ProPublica covered 2013-2018. The Physician Transparency Index extends through 2024, adds risk scoring, industry promiscuity analysis, and interactive visualizations.
