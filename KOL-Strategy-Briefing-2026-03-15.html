Bysanti (Milsaperidone): What Clinicians Need to Know About the Newest Atypical Antipsychotic

Source: FDA approval press release, February 20, 2026 + Psychiatric Times coverage

Bysanti (milsaperidone) was FDA-approved on February 20, 2026, as a first-line therapy for acute manic/mixed episodes in bipolar I disorder and for schizophrenia in adults. Key clinical points:

  • Mechanism: New chemical entity demonstrating bioequivalence to iloperidone across the therapeutic dosing spectrum, but with a distinct molecular profile.
  • Dosing advantage: Tablets with a simplified titration schedule compared to iloperidone.
  • First-line designation: Unlike many atypicals that are adjunctive, Bysanti is approved as a first-line acute treatment for bipolar I manic/mixed episodes.
  • Pipeline: Phase III trial for adjunctive treatment-resistant MDD — results expected end of 2026.
  • Commercial launch: Expected Q3 2026.
