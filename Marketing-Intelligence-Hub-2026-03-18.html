Clinical & Regulatory Alerts

DEA Telehealth Prescribing — Special Registration Period

The DEA's telehealth prescribing flexibilities for Schedule II-V controlled substances continue under the temporary rule extension through 2026. Providers must still comply with state-specific telehealth prescribing requirements. Monitor for the permanent rule expected mid-2026.

FDA: Auvelity (dextromethorphan/bupropion) Post-Market Data

Real-world evidence supporting Auvelity for treatment-resistant depression gaining traction. 1-week onset of action messaging resonating with patients. Content opportunity: educate on newer rapid-acting antidepressants.

Florida Telehealth Expansion — SB 1694

Florida continues to refine telehealth practice standards. Established patient relationships can be formed via telehealth. Refresh's multi-location FL footprint positions well for in-state patients requiring initial in-person then telehealth follow-up.

Massachusetts Telehealth Parity Law (Renewed)

MA telehealth parity law ensuring insurance reimbursement for telehealth at in-person rates remains active through 2026. Favorable for Refresh's 2 MA locations.

Texas SB 1107 — Telehealth Practice Updates

Texas telehealth regulations require an initial in-person or synchronous audiovisual evaluation for new patients before prescribing controlled substances. DFW locations provide the in-person touchpoint for subsequent telehealth management.

CMS 2026 Telehealth Reimbursement Updates

Medicare telehealth flexibilities extended. While most Refresh patients are commercial insurance, CMS policy signals influence commercial payer decisions. Audio-only mental health visits remain covered.

FDA

  • • Cannot promote medications for off-label uses in advertising
  • • Avoid "cure" or "guaranteed" outcome language for psychiatric conditions
  • • Patient testimonials about specific medications are regulated
  • • Fair balance: mention risks alongside benefits when discussing treatments

DEA

  • • Schedule II controlled substances (stimulants) have strict telehealth prescribing rules
  • • Temporary telehealth flexibilities extended but monitor for permanent rule
  • • Do not advertise easy access to controlled substances
  • • Avoid language suggesting stimulants will be prescribed without thorough evaluation

HIPAA & Social Media

  • • Never share patient information, even de-identified, without explicit consent
  • • Patient testimonials require written HIPAA authorization
  • • Screenshots of patient messages/reviews need consent for reposting
  • • Staff should not discuss patients on personal social media

State Telehealth Laws

  • • FL: Established patient relationship can form via telehealth; verify before controlled substance prescribing
  • • MA: Telehealth parity law active — full reimbursement parity
  • • TX: Initial in-person or synchronous A/V required before controlled substance prescribing
  • • Always specify which states you're licensed to practice in

Voice & Tone

Warm, approachable, professional. Evidence-based but never clinical-sounding. Empowering and stigma-reducing. Never fear-mongering or clickbaity.

Do

  • "You deserve support that fits your life"
  • "Research suggests..." / "Studies show..."
  • "Talk to a board-certified psychiatrist"
  • "We're here when you're ready"
  • "Managing symptoms effectively"
  • "Evidence-based treatment options"
  • "Your mental health journey"

Don't

  • "Suffering from..." / "Struggling with..."
  • "Cure your depression" / "Fix your anxiety"
  • "Don't wait — it will only get worse"
  • "Get pills fast" / "Easy prescriptions"
  • "You're broken" / deficit language
  • "Limited time offer" / urgency tactics
  • "This changed my life" (testimonial-style)
