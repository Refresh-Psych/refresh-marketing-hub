Treatment 1: Pregabalin for Treatment-Resistant Anxiety/Depression

Dimension Details
Drug Name Pregabalin (Lyrica)
FDA Approvals Fibromyalgia, neuropathic pain, partial-onset seizures
Off-Label Use Generalized anxiety disorder, social anxiety, adjunctive for TRD
Mechanism of Action Binds alpha-2-delta subunit of voltage-gated calcium channels → reduces excitatory neurotransmitter release (glutamate, norepinephrine, substance P)
Evidence Base RCTs for GAD (WFBP recommends as first-line for GAD); case reports for TRD augmentation
Key Study PMC12126151 — GAD Meta-Analysis & RCT Evidence
Patient Population GAD patients failing SSRIs/SNRIs; TRD augmentation candidates; chronic pain + anxiety overlap
Dosing Range 150–600 mg/day divided BID-TID; titrate 75 mg/day q3–7 days
Side Effects Sedation (20–30%), dizziness, weight gain, ataxia, impaired concentration, potential for misuse (Schedule V)
Withdrawal Risk Yes — rebound anxiety, insomnia, dysphoria on abrupt discontinuation. Taper over 7–14 days minimum.
Telehealth Feasibility HIGH ✓ — Can safely initiate and monitor via telehealth; no in-person requirement
Recommendation YES — IMPLEMENT FOR REFRESH

Consider as second-line for GAD and augmentation strategy for TRD. Cost-effective, well-tolerated, strong evidence base.

Treatment 2: Pramipexole for Treatment-Resistant Depression

Dimension Details
Drug Name Pramipexole (Mirapex)
FDA Approvals Parkinson's disease, restless legs syndrome
Off-Label Use Augmentation for treatment-resistant unipolar and bipolar depression
Mechanism of Action D3-preferring dopamine agonist → enhances dopaminergic transmission in mesolimbic reward pathway; modulates serotonin/norepinephrine circuits
Evidence Base Open-label studies, retrospective cohort studies showing efficacy over 24 weeks; growing clinical interest
Key Study ScienceDirect — Pramipexole Augmentation in Depression
Patient Population TRD patients with prominent anhedonia, motivational deficits, psychomotor retardation; bipolar depression; depression with negative symptoms
Dosing Range 0.5–5 mg/day; typically start 0.125–0.25 mg/day, titrate by 0.125 mg every 5–7 days
Side Effects Major Risk: Impulse control disorders (gambling, hypersexuality, compulsive spending) in 5–10% of patients; nausea, orthostatic hypotension, hallucinations, somnolence
Monitoring Requirements Baseline and ongoing assessment for impulse control disorders; orthostatic vital signs; dopamine agonist ergotism screening (rare but critical)
Telehealth Feasibility MODERATE ⚠️ — Requires careful monitoring for impulse control disorders. Consider periodic in-person visits or collateral contact with partner/family.
Recommendation CONSIDER FOR SELECT REFRESH PATIENTS

Best for: TRD with anhedonia who have failed standard augmentation strategies; patients without personal or strong family history of impulse control disorders. Requires robust informed consent and ongoing monitoring.

Treatment 3: Low-Dose Naltrexone (LDN) for Depression/Anxiety

Dimension Details
Drug Name Naltrexone (ReVia, generic)
FDA Approvals 50 mg daily for alcohol use disorder and opioid use disorder
Off-Label Use (LDN) 1–4.5 mg daily for depression, anxiety, fibromyalgia, chronic fatigue syndrome, autoimmune disorders
Mechanism of Action At low doses (LDN), transiently blocks opioid receptors 4–6 hours → compensatory upregulation of endorphins and enkephalins; anti-inflammatory via TLR4 antagonism; modulates neuroinflammation
Evidence Base Emerging — case series, small RCTs, growing clinical interest in pain-mood-inflammation nexus. Strong mechanistic rationale.
Patient Population Depression/anxiety with comorbid chronic pain, fibromyalgia, autoimmune disease; fatigue-predominant presentations; patients seeking non-traditional approaches
Dosing Range 1–4.5 mg once daily at bedtime; typically compounded pharmacy product (standard 50 mg tablets not suitable); take in capsule form
Side Effects Minimal at LDN doses — vivid dreams (30%), mild insomnia, nausea (transient), GI upset. Usually well-tolerated and time-limited.
Contraindications ABSOLUTE: Current or recent opioid use (will precipitate withdrawal). Requires minimum 7–10 day opioid washout prior to initiation.
Telehealth Feasibility HIGH ✓ — Easily initiated and monitored remotely; minimal adverse event risk; patient-friendly
Recommendation WORTH EXPLORING FOR REFRESH

Growing evidence base, excellent safety profile, strong patient interest in integrative approaches, addresses inflammation hypothesis of depression. Partner with compounding pharmacy for proper LDN formulation. Consider for patients with pain-mood comorbidity or those seeking add-on to standard antidepressants.
